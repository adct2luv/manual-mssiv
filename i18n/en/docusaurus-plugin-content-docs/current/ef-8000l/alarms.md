# Emergency Alarm Features

## 1. Fire Sensor Alarm

When the sensor detects abnormally high temperature (≥ 62°C ± 5°C):
- A loud alarm sounds
- **The door unlocks automatically** for evacuation

:::caution
- **Direct sunlight for long periods** may trigger the sensor
- Even in "Double Lock" mode, the door **opens automatically** when temperature is high
- When temperature returns to normal, the lock resumes normal operation
:::

## 2. Intrusion Alarm

When the lock detects forced entry from inside or outside, the alarm activates in pulses.

**To cancel:**
- Remove the batteries, OR
- Enter a registered PIN / Card / Fingerprint

:::note
If temperature is still high (Fire Alarm active), the Intrusion Alarm cannot be cancelled.
:::

## 3. Anti-Prank Alarm & 1-Minute Lock

After **5 consecutive failed** PIN / Smart Key / Fingerprint attempts:
- Alarm sounds
- Lock is disabled for **1 minute**

After 1 minute, the lock returns to normal operation.

## 4. Deadbolt Jammed Alarm

While opening or closing, if the **deadbolt is stuck or improperly placed**, the alarm sounds **"beep-beep-beep"** 3 times.

:::caution
If the alarm continues or the deadbolt operates abnormally, contact a local installer. Repair costs may apply.
:::

## 5. Replace Battery Alarm

During normal operation, if the **battery light comes on** with sound **"beep-ree-reep"**, the battery is low. **Replace all batteries immediately.**

## 6. Emergency Key (Emergency Key version only)

Use when the lock malfunctions or batteries are dead.

**Steps:**
1. **Open the key hole cover** at the top of the outer handle (push right)
2. **Insert the emergency key** into the hole, turn from **right to left**
3. When you hear **"tik"**, the deadbolt has moved back to its default position — remove the key
4. **Turn the handle downward** to open the door
5. **Push the key hole cover back** to close

:::warning
Store the emergency key in a safe but accessible place, not near the front door.
:::
